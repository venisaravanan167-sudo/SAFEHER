Women Safety Laws in India

Know your rights and legal protection

IPC Section 354 – Assault or Outraging Modesty

This law protects women from physical harassment or assault intended to insult their dignity. Any act like touching without consent, attacking or using force against a woman can be punished under this section. Punishment may include imprisonment and fines.

IPC Section 354A – Sexual Harassment

Sexual harassment includes unwanted physical contact, sexual comments, showing pornography without consent, or asking for sexual favors. The punishment may include imprisonment up to three years and a fine.

IPC Section 376 – Rape Law

IPC Section 509 – Insulting Modesty of a Woman

This law punishes any person who insults a woman's modesty through words, gestures or actions. Examples include verbal harassment, obscene comments, or gestures intended to offend a woman. Punishment may include imprisonment and fines. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005

This law protects women from domestic abuse within households. Domestic violence includes physical, emotional, sexual, verbal and economic abuse. Women can seek protection orders, financial support, residence rights and child custody under this law. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace Act, 2013

This law protects women from harassment at workplaces. Every organization must create a complaints committee to handle harassment cases. The aim of the law is to ensure safe and respectful working environments for women. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961

Demanding or giving dowry during marriage is illegal in India. This law punishes people who demand dowry and protects women from dowry harassment and abuse.

Child Marriage Prohibition Act, 2006

This law prohibits marriage of girls below 18 years of age and boys below 21 years. It aims to protect young girls from forced or early marriage.
