Internal Complaints Committee (ICC)

Ensuring Safe and Respectful Workplaces

About ICC

The Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) is a mandatory body created under the Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013. Its main purpose is to protect women from sexual harassment at the workplace and provide a safe environment for employees.

Every organization with 10 or more employees must establish an ICC to receive complaints and conduct investigations regarding workplace harassment.

Objectives of ICC

  • Prevent sexual harassment in the workplace.
  • Create a safe and respectful work environment.
  • Provide a fair and confidential complaint mechanism.
  • Support victims of harassment.
  • Promote gender equality and dignity in workplaces.

What Does ICC Do?

  • Receives complaints related to workplace harassment.
  • Conducts confidential investigations.
  • Ensures fair hearings for both parties.
  • Collects evidence and statements.
  • Recommends disciplinary or legal action to employers.
  • Maintains confidentiality during the investigation.
  • Provides awareness and training programs on workplace safety.

Structure of the ICC

  • A senior woman employee acts as the Presiding Officer.
  • At least two employee members committed to women safety.
  • One external member from an NGO or legal background.
  • At least 50% members must be women.

How to File a Complaint

  • A complaint must be submitted within 3 months of the incident.
  • The complaint can be submitted in writing or through email.
  • The ICC will start the inquiry process.
  • The investigation should normally be completed within 90 days.
  • Strict confidentiality is maintained during the process.
